The Bank v1 — Design Notes
Live ingredient catalogue with price-movement as the story. Top-bar pulsing Live indicator, eyebrow "Live · last update 07:14" — the surface tells you immediately that it's a real-time view, not yesterday's snapshot. The page title is just The Bank — definite article, italic-gold, treating it as a place not a feature.

Pending confirmation strip surfaces the auto-population intelligence. Three new ingredients waiting on chef confirmation, with the system's honest admission ("one the system isn't sure about"). Action lives in Inbox where the chef does other confirmation work — keeps The Bank focused on reading, not processing.

Each row tells the price-movement story at three resolutions: the 30-day sparkline shows the shape (steady creep, sudden spike, flat), the price + unit shows the current absolute, the arrow + percentage shows direction and magnitude. Up movements in price-up burnt red, down in healthy green, flat in muted grey. The chef can read each row in under two seconds.

Sort defaults to Movement — what's moved most, biggest changes first. Stable ingredients drop down the list. The chef opening The Bank most often wants to know what's changed, not browse alphabetically.

"Just in" highlighting on freshly-updated rows. Gold-bg row with thin gold left bar plus a "JUST IN" pill near the ingredient name. Lamb shoulder in the mockup shows this — auto-banked from this morning's Aubrey invoice. The chef sees freshness without having to think about it.

Multi-supplier badge surfaces sourcing optionality. Ingredients you buy from 2+ suppliers (tahini, olive oil) get the small "2 SUPPLIERS" badge. Clicking the row opens a comparison view (future v2) — where can you get this cheaper, where's the quality been better. 18 multi-sourced ingredients across the catalogue per the KPI strip.

Looking Ahead surfaces cross-pattern intelligence. Tahini up at both suppliers = market move, prescriptive ("worth stocking what you'll need"). Tomato & cucumber down together = seasonal opening, opportunistic ("good moment to push a salad-led starter"). The system reading patterns the chef wouldn't catch row-by-row.
