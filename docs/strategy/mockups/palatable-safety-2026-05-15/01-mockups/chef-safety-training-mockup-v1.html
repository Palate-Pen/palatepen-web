A Note On Why This Matters
Public liability insurers, EHO inspectors, and Natasha's Law all rely on documented training records. When something goes wrong, the first question is "was the person trained?" — and proof is in this log.
Important — Your Responsibility
Palatable is a record-keeping tool. The food business operator remains fully responsible for compliance with UK food safety law, including the Food Safety Act 1990, Regulation (EC) 852/2004, Natasha's Law, and all guidance from the Food Standards Agency and your local authority. Palatable does not provide legal advice or certification, does not replace EHO inspection, and does not guarantee compliance. We keep the records straight. The decisions, the kitchen, and the responsibility are yours.
Training Records Sub-Page — Design Notes
The destination Quick Action #4 goes to. Same pattern as the rest — breadcrumb, two-column layout, FSA Reference Strip and Expiring panel in the right column, liability footer.

Four summary cards at the top. Active Staff (8, healthy-green) · Expiring Soon (2, attention-amber) · Expired (1, urgent-red) · Total Certificates (24, neutral). At-a-glance compliance temperature for the whole team. Click any card to filter the staff list below.

Three buttons in the actions bar. Export Records (CSV / PDF for EHO Mode) · Add Certificate (attach a PDF of a course completion certificate) · Add Staff Member (primary action — adds new staff and prompts to set required certifications based on role).

Each staff member is a card. Avatar with initials in gold · name in Lora · role in italic Lora · "Since 2019" since-date · overall status pill on the right (Compliant green / Action Needed amber / Expired red / Onboarding amber). Below, a stack of certificate rows.

Certificate rows have colour-coded status. Valid (healthy-green left border) · Expiring (attention-amber, within 60 days) · Expired (urgent-red, past expiry). Each row: icon · cert name · provider/method · status text · expiry date in mono font. The chef can see at a glance who needs what.

Four worked-example staff. Jack (head chef, all valid — Level 3, allergen, HACCP) · Sam (sous chef, allergen expiring in 31 days) · Maya (CdP, food hygiene expired 8 days ago — urgent) · Danny (commis, new starter, allergen due in 14 days from induction). Shows the four standard states: fully compliant, expiring soon, expired, onboarding.

FSA Reference Strip in right column. Three links: Online food safety training (free) — this is the FSA's actual free training, which we direct chefs to · Allergen training requirements · Food safety management requirements. The FSA's free online training is genuinely good — pointing to it is part of being a credible record-keeping tool, not pretending to replace external certification bodies (CIEH, Highfield, etc.).

Expiring & Expired panel. Quick view of who needs action. Sorted by urgency — Maya first (−8 days, urgent red pill), Danny next (14 days, attention), Sam (31 days, attention). One tap on a row jumps to the staff card.

Why-This-Matters note. Italic Lora gold-bar callout: "Public liability insurers, EHO inspectors, and Natasha's Law all rely on documented training records. When something goes wrong, the first question is 'was the person trained?' — and proof is in this log." Sets the stakes without being alarmist.

The cross-shell loop. Training data flows to: (1) Looking Ahead on Chef Home as a Get-Ready tag ("Maya's hygiene cert expires in 8 days — book renewal"), (2) EHO Mode 90-day export as documented compliance, (3) Inbox notifications 30/14/7/0 days before expiry, (4) Manager Home as a multi-site staff compliance score (multi-site tier). One certificate, four surfaces.

What's NOT in v1. Training course delivery (we link to FSA free training and external providers — we don't deliver courses ourselves). Quizzes / assessments. SCORM e-learning module. Direct CIEH/Highfield API integration (chefs upload PDF certificates manually for now). Photo signatures.
