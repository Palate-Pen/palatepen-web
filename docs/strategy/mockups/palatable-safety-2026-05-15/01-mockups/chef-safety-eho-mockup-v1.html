EHO Visit Page — Design Notes
This is the most strategically important page in the Safety module. When an EHO walks through the door unannounced — every chef's gut-drop moment — the chef opens this page and the entire 90 days of quiet record-keeping becomes useful in 30 seconds. The whole Safety v1 product justifies itself in this one moment.

Command Header — dark, urgent, gold-accented. Unlike every other Safety surface (paper background, calm), this page leads with the ink-black command bar to signal: "this is the moment that matters." The "Export 90-Day Bundle" button is gold-filled — the biggest, most obvious action on the page. "PDF · 47 pages · 4 seconds to generate" subtitle tells the chef the magic is real and instant.

Visit Status row with live timer. Healthy-green left border, pulsing dot (animated), "Visit In Progress · Started 11:08 · Catherine Reid, Hackney Council EHO · Routine inspection". Live elapsed timer in JetBrains Mono on the right (00:24:18). This is the only animated element on the page — deliberate, calm signal that the visit is live, the records are tracking. Not anxious. Just present.

Compliance Posture — 5 metric cards. 81/90 days logged (healthy) · 412 probe readings all within FSA limits (healthy) · 68/68 deliveries logged (healthy) · 3 Sunday cleaning gaps (attention amber) · 1 expired training cert (urgent red). Honest posture, not inflated. The chef sees what the EHO will see. The two flags are real — the cleaning gaps and the expired training — and the page tells the truth about them. This builds trust both with the user and with the inspector. EHOs are trained to spot inflated dashboards. We don't do that.

Evidence Grid — 10 tiles. HACCP/SFBB Plan · Daily Diary · Temperature Records · Supplier Deliveries · Allergen Records · Cleaning Schedule · Training Records · Incident Log · Waste Log · Registration & History. Each tile pulls from a different part of Palatable (Recipes, Suppliers, Safety subpages, Settings). Each shows a status pill (In Place / Live / Compliant / Tracked) — colour-coded against the metric beneath. The two flagged tiles (Cleaning, Training) carry attention-amber backgrounds so the chef knows where the conversation will go before the EHO gets there. Every tile is tappable to drill into the underlying record.

The exported PDF. "PDF · 47 pages · 4 seconds to generate". Bundles: cover page with FSA reg number and current FHRS rating, executive summary (one-pager), HACCP plan, 90 days of daily diary entries, all probe readings, all deliveries with supplier temps, allergen map, cleaning schedule sign-offs, training records with expiry dates, incident log with corrective actions, waste log. Each section cites the relevant FSA guidance. Includes the Palatable liability footer on every page so the inspector sees we don't claim to certify anything.

Inspector Details card on the right. Four editable fields — Name, Authority, ID Shown, Visit Type. The chef fills these in during the first minute of the visit. The data becomes part of the inspection record automatically and gets stamped on the 90-day export PDF.

Live Visit Log — the killer feature. Sticky positioned on the right, scrollable. Chef types events as they happen ("EHO asked for HACCP plan", "EHO walked the walk-in", "EHO flagged the warm Fridge 2"). Four tag types: Arrival (ink black), Observed (gold), Requested (attention amber), Note (paper warm). Timestamped automatically. Auto-saved. Becomes the official chef's record of the visit, which can be cross-referenced later if any findings are disputed.

The seven worked-example log entries tell the story of a realistic 24-minute inspection: arrival, intro, HACCP request, diary walkthrough, walk-in fridge inspection, Fridge 2 flag identified by EHO, training records request. Show the chef the kind of detail to capture without prescribing too much.

Input row at the bottom of the log. Text input + tag dropdown + Add button. Stays accessible while the rest of the log scrolls. Chef types one-handed while answering the EHO's questions.

FSA Reference Strip — different framing here. "FSA Guidance — For the Inspector". Acknowledges this page is partly for the EHO, not just the chef. Notes that the export bundle includes FSA citations alongside each record. Four links: Food safety management · SFBB · How food hygiene ratings work (NEW — relevant for an EHO interaction specifically) · FSA Here to Help.

Cross-shell loops. When the chef hits "Visit In Progress" the first time (a manual toggle at the top, not shown in this mockup), it: (1) freezes any in-flight edits to the HACCP plan, (2) takes a snapshot of all current records (so anything edited later doesn't change what was shown to the EHO), (3) routes to Manager Home as a critical alert, (4) routes to the Inbox, (5) generates the export PDF in the background so it's ready instantly. When the chef hits "End Visit" (also not shown), it: (6) requests a quick rating note (good visit / minor follow-up / formal issues raised), (7) auto-creates Looking Ahead items if the EHO flagged anything (e.g. "Address Sunday cleaning gap by next visit"), (8) emails the visit log + export PDF to the owner.

What this means strategically. Every chef has an EHO visit story. The bad ones are folklore in the kitchen industry. Most include the line "we couldn't find the records." This page is the answer to that line. The whole 90 days of Safety logging exists for this moment. We need this page in every demo of Palatable — it's the most emotional sell. Owners will pay £20/month for the Safety tier just to have this page ready, even if they never have an EHO visit.

What's NOT in v1. No live video link to a remote consultant during the visit (out of scope). No direct API to local authority systems (LAs don't have one). No automatic FHRS score prediction (legally risky — we don't claim to predict outcomes). No bots that suggest answers to inspector questions during the visit. The chef talks to the inspector. We hold the records.
