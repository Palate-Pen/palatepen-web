Every UK food business is legally required to keep a documented food safety management system based on HACCP principles. Palatable is built around the FSA's Safer Food, Better Business (SFBB) framework — the diary, the four Cs (cross-contamination, cleaning, chilling, cooking), and the records you need to show an EHO. The official guidance is always one tap away.

Safety v1 — Design Notes
The wedge: replace the paper SFBB diary every UK kitchen is legally obliged to keep. Not HACCP wizard, not full allergen labels, not audit reports. Just the daily record-keeping, done well, integrated with what Palatable already knows.

Three sections, one workflow. Opening Checks (chef walks in, logs fridge temps, probe cal, cleaning verified, handwash stations, pest visual). During Service (auto-logged — deliveries come from Deliveries tab, waste from Waste, probe readings logged by chef when cooking). Closing comes later in the day (hot holding, cooling logs, cleaning complete). The diary calendar at the bottom shows the last 12 weeks at a glance — green/amber/red dots tell the EHO story instantly.

Looking Ahead applied to safety. Same pattern as every other Palatable surface. "Fridge 2 temp has crept up 0.4°C average this week" — proactive maintenance flag. "EHO last visited 11 months ago" — the 12-month window opens 14 June, get ready. "Cleaning completed 18 of last 21 days, gaps on Sundays" — behavioural pattern, not just a check failure. Three Worth-Knowing / Get-Ready / Plan-For-It tags rotating just like the chef shell home.

EHO Mode is the killer feature. Inspector shows up unannounced — one tap produces a clean 90-day PDF bundle covering everything (opening checks, deliveries with supplier temps, probe readings, cleaning, waste, training records, incidents). The 81/9 stats reassure the chef that what they're producing is solid. Replaces the binder of paper SFBB diary sheets that everybody loses or forgets to file.

Zero re-entry from existing data. Aubrey delivery? Already in Deliveries tab — auto-logged here with chilled temp. Lamb shoulder probe? Pulled from Prep tab when chef recorded the core temp. Waste? From the existing Waste log. The promise: "You're already doing this work. We just make it count as legal compliance."

Liability framing throughout. Footer on every page makes clear Palatable is a record-keeping tool, not a certification body. The user — the FBO — remains legally responsible. This is non-negotiable. Onboarding flow needs to make the user acknowledge this. Settings page has the full disclaimer + link to FSA's free SFBB pack. EHO export PDF includes the same footer so inspectors see we're not claiming to certify anything — the chef is.

The cross-shell loop. When a supplier delivery flags a temperature outside spec, that flows to Safety (logged as a temperature deviation) AND to Suppliers (logged against Aubrey's reliability score) AND to Looking Ahead on the Chef Home (flagged for the chef's attention). One event, three surfaces. The data lives once.

What's NOT in v1 (deliberately). No HACCP plan builder. No allergen label printer (Natasha's Law PPDS labels — that's v2). No incident root-cause analysis. No audit module. No training course delivery. No fridge sensor hardware. We build the wedge first, prove it, then expand.
