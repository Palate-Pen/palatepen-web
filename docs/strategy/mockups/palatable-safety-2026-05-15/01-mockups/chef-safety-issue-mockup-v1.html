Log an Issue Sub-Page — Design Notes
The destination Quick Action #2 goes to. Same pattern as Probe Reading — breadcrumb, two-column layout, full editable form on the left, FSA reference strip and recent log on the right.

Four issue types. Complaint, Allergy, Near-Miss, Illness. Each one has its own icon. Allergy is shown as the worked example because it's the highest-risk type and the most common reason a chef would open this page mid-service. The active state uses urgent-red border when the issue type is Allergy or Illness, attention-amber for Complaint or Near-Miss — different severity signal even before the chef picks the severity level.

Severity is honest, three levels. Low (minor complaint, no harm), Medium (minor reaction or risk), High (hospital, severe reaction). Italic helper text under the label: "honest assessment — high severity escalates." The system needs to know severity because high-severity allergy incidents have legal escalation paths (insurer, local authority, RIDDOR potentially).

All 14 UK regulated allergens as pills. Celery, Cereals/Gluten, Crustaceans, Eggs, Fish, Lupin, Milk, Molluscs, Mustard, Nuts (selected in this example), Peanuts, Sesame, Soya, Sulphites. Tap to toggle. Selected pills fill with urgent-red. This is the Natasha's Law list. Inline FSA reference link below the grid: "↗ FSA allergen guidance for food businesses" — direct link to food.gov.uk allergen page.

Dish picker reuses the Probe pattern. Three tabs (Today's Menu / Prep Items / Recipes Library), tap a dish to select, free-form fallback below for items not on a current menu. The selected dish (knafeh) shows in italic gold AND its allergen list — "contains nuts, milk, gluten" — so the chef sees the listed allergens at a glance and can confirm whether the reported allergen matches the dish.

When-it-happened is two fields. Date and Service (Dinner — 20:45). Both editable. Defaults to "now" but chef can backdate if the issue is logged after the fact.

The 'What Happened' textarea is the audit narrative. Worked example shows the level of detail needed: who, when, what was ordered, what was reported, what action was taken, what the guest did next. This is the witness statement equivalent. Chef should write more, not less.

Corrective actions as a checklist. Seven standard actions, three checked in the worked example. Stock isolation, allergen doc review, EHO notification, insurer notification — all unchecked because in this case they haven't happened yet. The list itself is a prompt: "have you done these things?" Chef can tick them as they go through the response.

The FSA Reference Strip is the new pattern. Gold left-bar callout in the right column, "FSA Guidance" eyebrow, "food.gov.uk — Authoritative Source" attribution. Three deep-linked references with external-link arrows: allergen guidance, reporting a food problem, food safety management. This pattern will be applied to every Safety sub-page with topic-relevant FSA links. The chef knows the official source is one tap away, always in the same place.

Recent Issues panel. Shows the last 3 issues logged, each with a coloured tag (Complaint, Near-Miss, Allergy), date, summary, and resolution status. Pattern matches the rest of Palatable — italic gold for dish names, healthy-green for resolved, attention-amber for open. Lets the chef see the pattern of their kitchen's incident history at a glance.

Liability footer stays. Identical wording on every Safety sub-page. Non-negotiable.

The cross-shell loop. A logged allergy incident appears in: (1) Chef Inbox as a critical notification, (2) Manager Home as a flagged operational issue, (3) Looking Ahead on Chef Home for the next 14 days as a learning prompt ("knafeh allergen handling — review with team"), (4) Recipes for the dish involved (incident count rises by 1), (5) the EHO Mode 90-day export as a documented incident. One event, five surfaces. Same data structure as a probe reading or a fridge temp — it just routes differently depending on what it is.
