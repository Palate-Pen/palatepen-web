Probe Reading Sub-Page — Design Notes
This is the destination Quick Action #1 goes to. Tap "Log a probe reading" from the Safety home, land here. Full editable form, integrated with the rest of Palatable's data.

Six check types. Cooking core, Hot holding, Cooling, Reheating, Chilled, Frozen. Each one carries its FSA target temperature beneath the label (≥75°C for cooking, ≥63°C for hot holding, etc.). When chef picks a type, the temperature input validates against that target and shows green/amber state. Removes the chef's mental burden of remembering targets.

The dish picker is the killer feature. Three tabs — Today's Menu (pulled from the live Dinner Menu in Menu Builder), Prep Items (pulled from the Prep tab batches), Recipes Library (full historical catalogue). Tap a dish, it's selected, the form records "lamb shoulder · from Dinner Menu May 2026" — full audit trail back to the menu version that was live that day. If an EHO asks "was that the same dish you served on the menu?" — we have the answer.

Free-form fallback. Below the dish list, an italic-placeholder input — "Or type the item — staff chicken thighs, birthday cake leftover…". The hint text explains when to use it: staff food, prep batch, returning leftovers, sub-recipe. Logged as free-form so the chef isn't blocked when something isn't on today's menu.

Temperature input is single-screen big. Mono-font, 32px, with °C/°F toggle. As the chef types, it validates against the selected check type's target — auto-green if above target, auto-amber if below. Beneath, the target-indicator line reads in plain English: "Target for cooking core: ≥ 75°C held for 30 seconds. You're 19°C above target." No ambiguity.

Notes is optional. Lora serif, 100px high, italic placeholder. Chef can capture context — "probed at thickest part, fat side down, batch of 4 reading 92-95°C." This becomes the audit narrative if anything ever needs investigating.

Right column shows today's log. All probe readings from today in chronological order. Each row: time · dish (with italic gold for menu-linked) · check type · cooker · reading. Mixed dish-linked and free-form entries — the chef sees the day's whole record at a glance. Includes fridge readings from the opening checks (showing how this surface connects to the wider Safety tab).

Tip card. "Probes need weekly calibration — boiling water or ice slurry. Last calibration logged Wed 13 May." Builds the calibration ritual into the chef's awareness without nagging.

Ghost card for future state. "Digital probe integration coming later — Thermapen, ETI, Navitas will sync readings straight in." Sets expectation that we'll receive Bluetooth data when ready, without making v1 wait for it.

Footer breadcrumb everywhere. "Safety › Log a Probe Reading" — clear path back. Same liability footer as the Safety home appears underneath (omitted in this mockup for brevity, but architecturally identical to chef-safety-mockup-v1).

What this means architecturally. Every dish probe reading is now linked to the menu version that was live when it happened. The audit trail becomes: "on Friday 15 May 2026 at 11:24, lamb shoulder from Dinner Menu (published 1 May) was probed at 94°C by Jack." If a menu changes, historical probe records still reference the old menu — they don't break. This is the data structure to ship in v1, even though digital probes come later.
