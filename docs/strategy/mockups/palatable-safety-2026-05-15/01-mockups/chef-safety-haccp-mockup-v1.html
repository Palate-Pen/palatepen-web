HACCP Wizard — Design Notes
The wizard is the v2 upgrade trigger. SFBB diary (v1) at the Kitchen tier (£79/site/mo). HACCP wizard + allergen labels move you to the Safety tier (£99). Compares to Food Alert charging £1,500-3,000 setup + £150-300/month for consultancy + software. We do it for a £20/month upgrade because the wizard does the consultant's work from data we already have.

Nine steps in a vertical sidebar on the left. Each step shows estimated time. Total ≈45 minutes if all data flows from Palatable. Each step name is short, the meta line tells the chef where the data is coming from ("Auto-populated from your menu", "FSA defaults pre-filled", "Maps to Safety tab"). The chef sees from the start that this isn't a blank-page exercise — most boxes are already filled.

Intro card explains the value. Gold left-bar callout: "Most kitchens spend £1,500–£3,000 on a HACCP consultant. The wizard does the equivalent work using your menu, suppliers, and recipes." Right side: "≈ 45 min Estimated Time" — sets expectation. The chef knows what they're committing to.

Pre-fill banner at the top of every step. Gold-bordered: "We've pre-filled what we know. Anything in gold is pre-filled — review, confirm, or change as needed." This is the central promise of the wizard. The chef is not entering data, they're confirming our work.

Step 1 (Business Profile) data sources. Trading name → Settings. Legal entity → Settings. FSA registration → Settings. Kitchen type → onboarding picker. Services offered → multi-select inferred from menu data (à la carte from Menus tab, bar from Bartender shell if installed, PPDS if labels module installed). Team size → Training Records (8 staff currently). Person responsible → Training Records (head chef with valid HACCP cert auto-selected).

Form components. Text inputs (with gold border when pre-filled and a "pre-filled" visual hint). Pickable cards for single-choice (kitchen type, team size). Check items for multi-select (services). Every field has a 1-line italic hint explaining what it's for and where the default came from.

Wizard footer. Save status on the left ("Saved as draft · 2 minutes ago" with healthy-green dot) — autosave runs every change so the chef can never lose work. Save & Exit + Continue to Step 2 on the right. The chef can leave the wizard at any point and come back later.

FSA strip is full-width here, 4 links in a 2x2 grid. Food safety management (the legal duty) · SFBB pack (the template) · Starting a food business (foundational) · FSA Here to Help (broad). Wider link grid because HACCP touches every part of the FSA's business guidance.

The liability footer is louder. Urgent-red border (not the usual warm-paper), label reads "Stronger Notice — HACCP & Legal Documents". Wording: "Palatable generates a draft HACCP plan from your data. This wizard does not replace professional food safety advice. We strongly recommend you have the generated plan reviewed by a qualified food safety adviser, your local authority Environmental Health team, or an accredited consultant before relying on it." This is non-negotiable. HACCP is a legal document, the stakes are higher than a daily diary, and we make that clear.

Step 2 onwards (not mocked but spec'd). Step 2 pulls every dish from the menu, auto-classifies hazard risk, asks chef to confirm. Step 3 walks the process flow per dish, suggests CCPs. Step 4 critical limits with FSA defaults (≥75°C cook, ≥63°C hot hold, etc.). Step 5 maps each CCP to a Safety tab check. Step 6 corrective actions from a library. Step 7 review cadence and sign-offs. Step 8 generates the PDF. Step 9 sets the annual reminder. Same wizard pattern throughout. Same pre-fill banner.

Output documents. Three artefacts produced at Step 8: (1) A formatted HACCP plan PDF — the legal document, dated and signed. (2) A chef's reference card — printable, for the kitchen wall, showing the CCPs and critical limits at a glance. (3) Cross-references back into the Safety tab so the chef sees which daily checks satisfy which HACCP CCP.

What's NOT in the wizard. No bespoke recipe risk assessment outside the wizard flow (built into Step 2 only). No supplier audit — we use existing Suppliers tab data. No on-site visit (we're software, not consultants). No certification — we generate the document, the chef takes ownership of it and gets it reviewed.

Pricing implication. The wizard is the trigger that moves a kitchen from Kitchen tier (£79) to Safety tier (£99). The £20/month uplift gives them: HACCP plan generation, Natasha's Law allergen label printing (v3), and unlimited document version history. Payback for the chef: one EHO visit they pass cleanly, one allergen incident handled correctly, one consultant invoice they don't have to pay.
