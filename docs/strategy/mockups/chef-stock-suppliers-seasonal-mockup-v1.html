Seasonal Sub-page v1 — Design Notes
Lives as a sub-page of Stock & Suppliers. Breadcrumb shows the path. Reference data, not authoring data — sits alongside The Bank and Suppliers as a lookup destination.

Four KPI cards at the top tell the chef what to care about today: "In Season Now" (37), "At Peak" (8), "Ending Soon" (3), "Coming Next Month" (11). Each KPI sub-line names specific things — "asparagus, wild garlic, rhubarb" — so the chef knows what to look at without scrolling the calendar.

The calendar itself is the main attraction. 12-month timeline across, ingredients down the left. Each row shows the ingredient's season as bars: light-gold for "in season", darker gold for "peak", warm amber for "ending soon". May (current month) is highlighted as a column with gold-bg tint and an underline.

"In your notebook" indicator shows on rows the chef has notebook entries about — sumac, stone fruit, aubergine, sea bream all get the small Cinzel pill. This is the bridge to Notebook's Coming Into Season cards: the system knows what the chef has worked on and surfaces it in context.

Status dot on the label tells the at-a-glance state: green for in-season, gold for peak, amber for ending soon, muted for out-of-season. Sub-line gives natural-language context ("Yorkshire forced ending · outdoor still good").

Hand-curated UK-focused dataset. 82 ingredients in v1 covering vegetables, fruit, herbs & aromatics, mushrooms, fish & seafood, game. Grows over time. Becomes a moat — the more accurate it is, the more valuable.
