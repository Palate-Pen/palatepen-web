Chef Notebook v1 — Design Notes
Different surface type, same design system. Notebook is the first non-dashboard surface — it's content-first not data-first. The design system holds: Cormorant Garamond + Cinzel + Jost, cream paper, gold accents, italic-gold for the noun that matters. But the structure is masonry-style entries rather than three-layer intelligence.

Four capture buttons in the page header. Voice (primary, gold-filled — most-used capture method on the kitchen floor), Photo, Sketch, Note. Voice gets the prominence because it's how a chef captures during service when hands are dirty.

The entries are the surface. Masonry grid of mixed entry types — voice memos (with waveform), photos, sketches, free-form notes — each tagged by dish/ingredient/concept. Shared-with-brigade indicator on entries that are visible to the team. Private entries (like "Thoughts on the new sous") stay private.

Sharing applies Kitchen tier and above. Pro tier (single user, single outlet) sees no sharing UI because there's nobody to share with. Kitchen+ sees the "shared" indicator and the sharing controls per entry. Opt-in per entry — chef chooses what's visible to brigade.

Coming Into Season is the forward-looking layer. Instead of "Looking Ahead" (which doesn't fit a creative surface), Notebook gets "Coming Into Season" — the seasonal produce calendar overlaid against past notebook entries. Sumac coming in next week + you have a saffron-cured trout sketch tagged sumac from February = surface this entry. The system links past creative work to present ingredient availability. This is forward-intelligence adapted to Notebook's character.

The voice in Coming Into Season is informative, not alarmist. "You worked on a saffron-cured trout with sumac in February" is observational — the system pointing out a connection, not telling the chef what to do. The chef decides whether to pick up the thread.
