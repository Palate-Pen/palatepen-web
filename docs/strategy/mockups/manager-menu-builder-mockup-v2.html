Menu Builder v2 — Editing Power Added
v2 adds the editing controls that turn this from a smart preview into a real design tool.

View mode toggle (top bar). Chef View shows menu engineering overlays — golden triangle highlight, Star/Puzzle labels, margin indicators if enabled. Customer View hides all of it and shows the menu exactly as a guest will see it. Same surface, two lenses. Critical because the overlays are useful for designing but would confuse a customer if accidentally printed.

Layout controls. Single column (current view, classic editorial), Two column (more dishes per page, useful for longer menus), Three column (drinks lists, all-day diner menus). Per-element toggles below — show prices on/off (some venues hide prices for tastings or guests), show descriptions, section dividers, allergen icons. Toggles use the v8 gold-fill switch pattern.

Engineering Overlays — separated section with explicit "internal cues" disclaimer. Golden triangle highlight, Star & Puzzle labels, margin %s, cost-per-cover — all toggleable independently. Auto-hidden when View Mode is Customer View, auto-hidden in print/PDF output. The chef can play with which signals they want to see while editing without polluting what guests will see.

Custom Template Upload — the "bring your own" option. Drag-drop zone accepts PDF, AI (Illustrator), Figma exports, PSD. Max 20MB. For kitchens with existing brand identity (Berber & Q has hand-illustrated menus designed by a studio) who want Palatable's costing intelligence but not Palatable's visual templates. Below the upload zone, a "Formatting guide" link opens a detailed how-to (separate page/modal not shown here) — page sizes, font safe-zones, dish placeholders, image specifications, what Palatable injects vs preserves.

The formatting guide tells uploaders: use page size A4 or US Letter portrait, leave a "dishes" placeholder area where Palatable will inject the menu content, supply fonts as embedded (or use Palatable defaults), name layers semantically (section-header, dish-name, dish-price), provide editable text frames where prices will live, supply CMYK colour profiles for print accuracy. The guide is the bridge between "design tool I already use" and "Palatable operational intelligence" — it makes adoption frictionless for studios and chefs with existing brand work.

The wedge holds. Even with a custom template uploaded, the Bank pulls live prices, menu engineering classifies dishes, the suggestions panel flags margin slips, the QR/print/web outputs all stay synced. The chef gets their visual brand AND our operational layer. No menu designer on the market offers that.
