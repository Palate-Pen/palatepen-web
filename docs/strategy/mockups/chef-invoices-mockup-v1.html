Invoices v1 — Design Notes
Write-and-workflow surface. Different again from Suppliers (write-and-reference) and The Bank (read-and-live). The chef opens this to do paperwork: review what came in, raise credit notes for discrepancies, track responses, reconcile against supplier accounts. Status is the primary mental axis — everything else is supporting context.

Status-led row layout. Six columns: supplier, date, total, status (with inline context), action, arrow. Each row's left-edge carries a coloured bar matching its status — urgent for discrepancies, gold for credits in flight, healthy for reconciled, muted for paid. Status pills are dot+label in Cinzel, status context underneath in italic Cormorant. Reads at three levels: the colour bar tells you something needs sorting, the status pill tells you what kind, the context tells you the specifics.

Inline credit note workflow. The discrepancy row's action button reads "Draft credit note" — one click into a guided draft flow with the discrepancy pre-filled. Once raised, status flips to Credit Note In Flight, the gold bar takes over, and the row tracks days-pending until reconciled. This is the chef-love feature.

Filter pills carry their counts. All (14) / Needs action (3) / In flight (2) / Reconciled (9). The chef sees their workload structure at a glance.

"Scan an invoice" quick action lives where it does on the hub. Top-right of the page header, same camera icon, mobile-first hint. The surface is also where the chef lands after scanning — closing the loop between capture and review.

Looking Ahead does paperwork forward-intelligence. Payment run forecasting (three invoices due, plus a pending credit that offsets it) and supplier behaviour patterns (Mediterranean's substitutions are creeping up — third credit in six weeks). Not "did you get this invoice yet" — the system spots patterns the chef wouldn't catch invoice-by-invoice.
