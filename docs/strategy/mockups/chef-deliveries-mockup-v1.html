Deliveries v1 — Design Notes
Day-grouped timeline, not a flat list. Each day is its own section with a header carrying day-level metadata ("Today · 3 deliveries · 2 arrived · 1 due soon"). Today's header has a healthy-green underline to mark it as the now-anchor; future days get the standard gold underline.

Five row states. Left-edge bar carries the colour: urgent for discrepancies (lamb today), healthy for arrived & matched, attention for due soon, gold for expected later today/tomorrow, muted for scheduled future. Status pill with dot + label plus inline italic context line — the chef reads it at a glance.

Five columns: Supplier + arrival window · Contents description · Value (with auto-banked indicator) · Status pill + context · Arrow. Dense but each piece does its job. Docket-attached pill on arrived rows signals the photo of the delivery slip is on file.

Future deliveries show estimated value in muted text ("~£390") with a "not yet ordered" / "est. from PO" sub-line. The chef knows what's coming and when, before it's confirmed.

Looking Ahead does operational planning. Monday's heavy meat order needs walk-in space cleared and pass-staffing — actionable advice, not just a number. Tuesday's order needs going in tonight before Tahini ticks again — connects the delivery to today's ordering deadline.
