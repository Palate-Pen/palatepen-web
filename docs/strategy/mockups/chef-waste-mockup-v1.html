Waste v1 — Design Notes
Smallest sub-page in scope, biggest single insight. The KPI strip alone tells the chef what they need: this week's total, this month's budget tracking, top category, worst day. Four numbers do most of the work.

"Log waste" lives where the other primary actions do. Top-right of the page header. The sub-line "takes 10 seconds" sets the expectation — this is not a paperwork burden, it's a quick capture (item, weight, reason). The faster it is to log, the more accurate the data.

Three attention cards with distinct severities: Urgent pattern (herbs walking the same path as last week), Recurring (Sundays still worst), Improving (aubergine halved — Working/Celebrating). The mix of forensic + positive signal matters here — waste data can be demoralising, so surfacing wins where they exist keeps the surface honest rather than just nagging.

Two-card analytics row. By Category (horizontal bars, biggest waste category on top, top one in attention amber) plus By Day of Week (bar chart, weekend in attention amber, dry caption "Sundays consistently 60% above the weekly average"). At-a-glance pattern recognition.

Recent Log is dense but honest. Each entry has a when, a what (item + weight), a why (the reason — "prepped too much for Wednesday service · wilted by close"), category, value. The "why" column is the most important — chef catches their own patterns by reading reasons across entries.

Looking Ahead does pattern intervention. "Weekend prep heading over again — worth scaling down 15%" and "Herb waste up, order pattern hasn't moved — worth comparing Mediterranean's order against this week's covers." The system not just reporting waste but suggesting where to intervene.
